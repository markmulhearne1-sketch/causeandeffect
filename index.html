~/cause-effect/thesis.txt
cause & effect is the independent research desk for global supply chain operators. weekly intelligence briefs. quarterly benchmark reports. a proprietary dataset spanning freight cost, inventory turns, lead times, and technology stack — contributed by the operators inside the world's largest supply chains, normalized and published by us. built on one belief: the industry that moves $25 trillion a year deserves a canonical source. today, it does not have one.

the best supply chain decisions
are made with the best data.

today, that data does not exist in one place. it lives in fragments — buried in consultancy reports, scattered across vendor dashboards, locked behind seven-figure subscriptions, or simply sitting unmeasured on someone's desk. every operator sees a sliver. nobody sees the whole. cause & effect aggregates it, normalizes it, and publishes it. weekly. for free.

the remaining 99% is locked inside vendor dashboards, consultancy engagements, and competitors' private books. every other trillion-dollar industry — semis, equities, energy, pharma — has an independent intelligence layer. supply chain does not. that is what we are building.

what we publish
01 // weekly
the monday brief.

every monday, 7am ET. what moved in global logistics last week, what it means for your operation, what to do about it. nine minutes. no filler.

02 // quarterly
benchmark reports.

freight cost per lane. inventory turns by vertical. lead-time variance. tech stack adoption. anonymized, normalized, cross-checked against the operator dataset. a reference no consultancy will give you for free.

03 // rapid
disruption deep dives.

when a port closes, a carrier fails, a region locks down, a tariff lands — we publish the operator's read within 48 hours. what happened, who is exposed, what the historical analogs say.

04 // dataset
the operator dataset.

a contributory benchmark. subscribers submit their own numbers anonymously, we normalize across industry and scale, and return the distribution. you see where you sit. nobody sees who you are.

the gap we are filling
the publishing void.

semis have SemiAnalysis. finance has Bloomberg. tech has Stratechery. supply chain has consultancy decks and vendor whitepapers. an industry moving $25T of value a year has no independent intelligence layer. that is not a market gap — that is a market error.

the data asymmetry.

the average operator makes routing, carrier, and inventory decisions worth $100M+ per year, informed by the same three vendor reports everyone else has read. the large tech-forward shippers have proprietary benchmarks. everyone else is flying with one eye closed.

the decision tax.

a conservative read: bad benchmarking costs the average enterprise shipper 3–7% of logistics spend annually. at the fortune 1000 level that is $40bn+ of value left on the table every year — not because the operators are wrong, but because the reference set does not exist.
